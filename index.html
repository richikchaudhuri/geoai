What is it

GEOAI detects damage on roads from ordinary smartphone photos. A citizen opens the RoadSide app, takes a picture of a road, and within seconds the system says whether the road is damaged, what kind of damage it is (crack, pothole, etc.), how severe it looks, and how confident it is in that answer. Photos with low confidence are routed to a human expert for review. Over time, the model learns from those corrections.

How it works
  1. Capture. RoadSide mobile app uploads a JPEG and GPS coordinates to Cloudinary.
  2. Queue. A Postgres trigger creates an assessments row in Supabase with status pending.
  3. Classify. An asyncio worker on an A5000 GPU claims a batch (FOR UPDATE SKIP LOCKED), runs the two-stage pipeline, and writes results back.
  4. Review. Anything below 80% confidence is flagged for an expert; corrections feed future fine-tuning.
  5. Surface. Civic authorities see the live hotspot map you're using right now.
The AI pipeline

A Qwen2.5-VL-7B vision-language model fine-tuned on RDD2022 and GAPs V2. Confidence is read from raw model logits — not from string-matching the response.

1
Binary detection
First-token softmax over the Normal/Distress class tokens. 76.5% accuracy on the Phase 1 baseline.
2
Type & severity
Geometric mean of per-token probabilities. Recognises longitudinal (D00), transverse (D10), alligator (D20), pothole (D40), block crack (D43) — plus zero-shot generalisation to unseen distress types via taxonomy injection.
